Why convert HEIC to PNG?

PNG (Portable Network Graphics) is lossless. Unlike JPG, which throws away image data to shrink file size, PNG preserves every single pixel exactly as it was. That makes PNG the right choice when image quality matters more than file size — graphic design work, screenshots, photo editing, printing, or any image you plan to edit further without accumulating compression artifacts.

PNG also supports a transparent background (alpha channel), which JPG cannot do. If you're pulling graphics, logos, or edited images out of an iPhone that happen to be in HEIC format, PNG is the only way to preserve that transparency.

HEIC to PNG, done privately in your browser

Most online HEIC converters upload your files to a server. That's slow, costs bandwidth, and means your personal images briefly sit on someone else's hardware. HEIC Quick does the entire decode-and-encode pipeline locally using a WebAssembly build of libheif. The browser tab reads your file, decodes the HEIC, and writes out a PNG — all without a single byte crossing the network.

This matters especially for PNG output because the people using PNG tend to be designers, developers, and professionals working with sensitive or unreleased visual assets. You shouldn't have to upload your client's branding to a random website just to change its format.

JPG vs PNG vs WebP — which should you pick?

JPG — smallest file, lossy compression, no transparency. Best for sharing and general use.

PNG — lossless, supports transparency, larger files. Best for editing, design, screenshots, and archival.

WebP — modern format, smallest of the three at similar quality, supports both lossy and lossless modes. Best for web publishing. Convert to WebP instead →

A note on file size

Expect your PNG outputs to be roughly 3–5x larger than the original HEIC files. HEIC uses a modern HEVC-based compression that's about twice as efficient as JPG; PNG uses old-school lossless compression. A 3 MB HEIC can easily become a 12–15 MB PNG. This is normal and expected — you're paying for perfect quality with disk space.

If file size matters more than perfect fidelity, convert to JPG or WebP instead.

Frequently asked questions

Why convert HEIC to PNG instead of JPG?

Choose PNG when quality matters more than file size — editing workflows, screenshots, graphics with text or sharp edges, or anything you'll process further. Every time you save a JPG it loses a bit of quality; PNG never does.

Does PNG support transparency from HEIC?

Yes. If the source HEIC has an alpha channel, HEIC Quick preserves it in the PNG. Most iPhone photos don't have transparency, but screenshots, edited images, and exported graphics may.

Are PNG files larger than HEIC?

Significantly — usually 3 to 5 times larger. That's the cost of lossless encoding vs. modern compression.

Is this free?

Completely. No signup, no limits, no watermarks. Open source on GitHub.

Does it preserve EXIF data?

PNG can carry some metadata, and HEIC Quick preserves the original capture date, camera model, and GPS coordinates where possible.
