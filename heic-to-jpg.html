Why convert HEIC to JPG?

Since iOS 11, iPhones and iPads save photos in HEIC (High Efficiency Image Container) by default. HEIC files are roughly half the size of JPG at the same visual quality, which is great for your iCloud storage — but terrible the moment you try to share those photos with anyone not on an Apple device.

Windows 10 and 11 can technically open HEIC files, but only after you install a paid codec from the Microsoft Store. Most photo editors, older software, content management systems, job application portals, printing services, and countless websites still reject HEIC outright. The fastest fix is to convert to JPG — the universal format that works literally everywhere.

How HEIC Quick is different

Nearly every "free HEIC to JPG" site works by uploading your photos to a server, converting them there, and sending them back. That means strangers' servers briefly hold your personal photos — your vacations, your family, your documents. Most of these sites also limit you to a handful of files per hour, plaster ads everywhere, and require signups.

HEIC Quick runs the entire conversion inside your browser using a WebAssembly build of libheif (the same decoder used by major Linux distributions). Your files never touch our servers. You can literally unplug your network cable after the page loads and conversion still works. No signup. No limits. No file size caps beyond what your device's memory allows.

How to convert HEIC to JPG (step by step)

  1. Drag your HEIC files onto the drop zone above — or click it to browse. You can select dozens of files at once.
  2. Make sure JPG is selected as the output format.
  3. Adjust the quality slider. 92% is a good default — visually indistinguishable from the original for almost all photos, with much smaller file sizes than 100%.
  4. Click Convert. Conversion runs locally; a progress indicator shows each file finishing.
  5. Click Download on each file, or Download All to get a ZIP of everything at once.

Frequently asked questions

How do I convert HEIC to JPG on Windows?

Drag your HEIC file onto the drop zone at the top of this page. The conversion runs entirely in your browser — nothing uploads to any server. Works on Windows 10, Windows 11, and any modern browser.

Is it safe to convert HEIC files online?

On HEIC Quick, yes. Your photos never leave your device. Most other "online" converters are not safe in the privacy sense — they upload your photos to their servers. Always check whether a converter runs locally (like this one) or server-side before uploading personal photos.

Does this work on iPhone and iPad?

Yes. Safari fully supports the WebAssembly features this tool uses. Tap the upload area and select from your camera roll.

Is there a file size or batch limit?

No artificial limits. The only practical constraint is your device's available memory. Most phones and laptops comfortably handle 50+ photos at once.

Will I lose image quality converting HEIC to JPG?

JPG is a lossy format, so there is always some compression. HEIC Quick lets you adjust quality from 60% to 100%. At 92%, the difference is visually imperceptible for almost all photos, while keeping file sizes reasonable.

Does it keep EXIF data like the date and location?

Yes — EXIF metadata including the original capture date, camera model, and GPS location (if present in the original HEIC) is preserved in the converted JPG.

Why is my photo library full of HEIC files in the first place?

Since iOS 11, iPhones default to HEIC because it uses roughly half the storage of JPG at the same quality. You can turn this off: Settings → Camera → Formats → Most Compatible. That switches future photos to JPG directly.
